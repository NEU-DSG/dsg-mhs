Subject Headings

Scholarly editors at the Massachusett's Historical Society assign subject headings to each entry in John Quincy Adams's diary. The headings describe the general content of JQA's daily journal-keeping. When aggregated, these subject headings can reveal an overview of JQA's focus: the subjects he most frequently thought about when we recorded his thoughts at the end of the day.

Subject Headings Count by Year

While a raw count of subjet headings indicates a sense of primacy, which subjects JQA considerd most important for his journal, it can also obscure the rhythms and cycles of life. Rather than aggregating subject headings by their total, which conveys an overview of decades-worth of writing, aggregating by year can highlight the shifts and movements in JQA's attention.

Animating the count of subject headings each year shows how JQA's attention focused and unfocused on different realms of work and home life.
